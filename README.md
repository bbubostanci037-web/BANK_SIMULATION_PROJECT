# BANK_SIMULATION_PROJECT
My project for DSA102 class.


The sustainability of a financial institution depends on its capacity to meet its deposit liabilities using available liquid 
cash resources (capital and interest-bearing loan recoveries). This project simulates the operational lifespan of a 
small-scale bank model to evaluate liquidity dynamics and cash flow thresholds. In the midterm phase, a simplified 
Python-based model validated basic cash flow and interest calculations under a static scenario. For the final phase, 
the system was re-engineered as a modular, Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) framework. This final design 
integrates customer risk profiling, dynamic interest calculations (such as credit-score-adjusted premiums), multi
branch accounting, and account state management (active, closed, defaulted). The prototype was evaluated against 
a 50-client dataset across three physical branches (Istanbul, Mersin, Gebze) over a 60-month time horizon. The 
simulation identifies critical liquidity trends, demonstrates OOP encapsulation and polymorphism, and suggests 
optimal pathways for physical expansion and risk mitigation.
